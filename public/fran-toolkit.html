How to Pitch a Brand by Email — Beginner Steps

Read this once top-to-bottom. Then use the Brand Finder tab to actually write & send.

The whole game in one sentence: Find a brand you genuinely like → find a real human's email → send a short, specific message that shows them one thing you can make for them → follow up twice. That's it.
1

Make a target list (15–20 brands)

Don't email randomly. Pick brands you'd actually use on camera — travel gear, hotels, skincare, snacks, apps. Small & medium brands reply the most; huge brands have agencies and ignore DMs.

Good starting pools: products already in your videos, brands other UGC creators tag, TikTok Shop products with a "commission" badge, and brands running lots of ads (they need content).

2

Find the right person's email

You want marketing / influencer / partnerships / brand — not customer support. Full how-to is in the Brand Finder tab, but the fast version:

  • Brand website → footer or "Contact" / "Press" / "Work with us" page.
  • Instagram/TikTok bio → often has a business email or a "collabs" link.
  • Guess the pattern: hello@brand.com marketing@brand.com partnerships@brand.com pr@brand.com
3

Write a subject line that gets opened

Short, specific, no hype. Examples:

  • "UGC content for [Brand] — travel creator"
  • "Quick idea for [Brand]'s TikTok"
  • "[Brand] x Fran — short-form video collab"

Avoid: "COLLAB?? 🔥🔥", "Amazing opportunity for you", anything that smells like spam.

4

Use the 5-part email structure

  1. Hook (1 line) — something specific & true about them ("Been using your X for every trip this year").
  2. Who you are (1 line) — "I'm Fran, a travel & lifestyle UGC creator."
  3. What you offer (1–2 lines) — the deliverable: "short-form videos you can use on your TikTok, ads, and website."
  4. Proof (1 line + link) — portfolio link. Let the work talk.
  5. Soft ask (1 line) — "Would it be helpful if I sent a couple of concepts?" Low pressure = more replies.

Keep the whole thing under ~120 words. Nobody reads long cold emails.

5

Follow up (this is where the money is)

Most replies come from follow-ups, not the first email. Be polite, not annoying:

  • Follow-up #1 — 3–4 days later. "Bumping this up in case it got buried 🙂"
  • Follow-up #2 — ~1 week after that. Add a new angle or a fresh idea, then stop.
6

Track everything

Use the Outreach Tracker tab. Log who you emailed, when, and the status. Aim for a steady number per week (e.g. 5–10). Volume + follow-ups = deals.

TikTok Shop & UGC — Everything a Beginner Needs

Plain-English crash course. No fluff.

First, know the difference

TermWhat it meansWho pays you & how
UGC (User-Generated Content)You make videos/photos of a product. The brand posts them (on their page, in ads, on their site). You're a content creator, not necessarily an influencer.The brand pays a flat fee per video (or gives free product). Your follower count barely matters.
Influencing / Creator postsYou post on your own account to your audience.Paid per post, based on your reach/engagement.
TikTok Shop AffiliateYou promote products sold on TikTok Shop with a special link/tag.Commission on every sale made through your video — passive-ish income.

Most beginners make money fastest with UGC (flat-fee) + TikTok Shop affiliate (commission). You can do both.

TikTok Shop Affiliate — how to start

  1. Eligibility (US, 2026): 18+ and 1,000+ followers to join. Between 1K–5K followers you're in the Creator Pilot Program (~30 days): only top-rated shops' products, max 5 product videos + 3 LIVEs/week. At 5,000+ followers the full product marketplace unlocks.
  2. Turn it on: TikTok app → Profile → menu (☰) → Creator tools → TikTok Shop for Creators / Affiliate. Follow the sign-up.
  3. Find products: open the Affiliate marketplace ("Add products" / "Showcase"). Filter by category (travel, beauty, home) and by commission %.
  4. Request free samples: many sellers offer free product to creators — request through "Free samples" so you don't pay out of pocket.
  5. Make the video → tag the product → post. When someone buys via your tag, you earn commission.
Pick products you'd actually recommend and that fit travel/lifestyle. Trust converts; random dropshipping junk kills your reach and credibility.

What makes a TikTok Shop / UGC video convert

  • Hook in 1–2 seconds — show the problem or the "wow" instantly.
  • Native, not an ad — film like a friend showing you something, not a commercial.
  • Show it in use — packing the bag, applying the product, real hands, real light.
  • One clear benefit — "fits a week of clothes in a carry-on," not ten features.
  • Soft CTA — "it's on my page / tagged below" beats "BUY NOW."
  • Good audio + captions — trending sound (when allowed) and on-screen text.

Common beginner mistakes

  • ✗ Waiting until you have a "big enough" following. UGC doesn't need one.
  • ✗ Pitching with no portfolio. Make 3 spec videos for brands you love first (even unpaid samples).
  • ✗ Emailing support@ or a generic form. Find the marketing/partnerships person.
  • ✗ Sending long, generic, copy-paste emails. Personalize the first line every time.
  • ✗ Never following up. Most yeses come from follow-up #1 or #2.
  • ✗ Forgetting to disclose #ad / #gifted. Required by the FTC and by brands.
